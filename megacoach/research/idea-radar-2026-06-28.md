### Theme 1: US LNG "Global Swing Supplier" ⭐ TOP PICK

**What's NEW this week vs prior round:** NOT in last week's scan. The Strait of Hormuz has been closed since Feb 28, 2026 following US-Israel strikes on Iran — this is a geopolitical energy shock with no parallel since the 1970s oil embargo. US LNG is now the only available swing supplier globally. This is the freshest major catalyst in this scan.

**One-line Thai thesis:** ฮอร์มุซปิด 4 เดือน — US กลายเป็น Swing Supplier LNG โลกเพียงรายเดียว ขณะ Golden Pass เพิ่งเปิดตัวและ Pipeline ก๊าซสู่ Gulf Coast คือคอขวดถัดไป

**Upstream bottleneck / angle:** US export terminal capacity is now maxed out (Golden Pass Terminal 9 started April 2026). The NEXT binding constraint is US intrastate gas pipelines moving production from Appalachia/Permian to Gulf Coast export terminals. Williams Companies (WMB) explicitly flagged this in May 2026. Pipeline throughput expansion takes 2-3 years — creating a durable earnings tailwind for operators who own existing capacity.

**Evidence:**
- Strait of Hormuz closed Feb 28, 2026 (US-Israel military strikes on Iran). 25% of world's seaborne oil and **20% of world's LNG** transited this strait. >80 million tonnes/year of LNG now stranded. *(Source: Wikipedia — 2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis; Wood Mackenzie press release 2026)*
- Brent crude surged past **$120/bbl** as of late Q1 2026; Bloomberg scenario analysis projects $200/bbl under severe closure scenario. *(Source: Bloomberg "Iran War Hormuz Closure Oil Shock" 2026)*
- **Golden Pass LNG** (Terminal 9) shipped first cargo April 2026, becoming the 9th US LNG export facility. US export forecast raised to **17.0 Bcf/d** for full year 2026. *(Source: EIA Today in Energy Apr 2026)*
- Three US LNG projects reached FID in 2026 YTD: CP2 Phase 2, Commonwealth LNG, Delfin FLNG 1 — **31 bcm/yr combined nameplate capacity**. *(Source: Global LNG Hub Mar 2026; IEA)*
- **Cheniere Energy (LNG)** Q1 2026: record LNG exports; EBITDA guidance raised to **$7.25-7.75B**. Committed $4.69B Sabine Pass expansion via Bechtel contract. Citi raised price target to **$330 from $280**. *(Source: Columbia CGEP; Wall Street Journal Apr 2026; Investing.com)*
- **Williams Companies (WMB)** May 2026: "Why US LNG's next big test is pipelines, not supply" — identified gathering/transport infrastructure as the binding constraint now that liquefaction capacity is spoken for. *(Source: Williams.com May 2026)*
- European and Asian buyers rushing to sign long-term US LNG supply contracts; ~90% of prior Hormuz LNG was destined for Asia. *(Source: NPR/NH Public Radio Apr 14, 2026)*
